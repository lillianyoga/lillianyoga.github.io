Bio

Lillian found yoga at an early age and immediately recognized its many benefits. She fell in love with the practice and the way it enhanced her other physical activities, such as mountaineering, rock and ice climbing, running, and tennis. She completed the 200-hour training at Yoga Vida in 2018 and further studied under the guidance of her mentor, Sangeeta Vallabhan.

Lillian’s classes are athletic and challenging, yet balanced, safe, and accessible to all. She believes that a mindful yoga practice extends beyond the mat and infuses everything we do. With that in mind, Lillian aims to have people leave her studio feeling stronger, calmer, and more connected to the world around them.

In 2018, Lillian climbed Mount Everest, completing the Seven Summits. She has run the New York Marathon ten times, has qualified for and run the Boston Marathon twice, and regularly plays tennis. She is the treasurer of The Practice Coalition, a non-profit dedicated to providing trauma-informed yoga and to taking care of the teachers who offer it.

Class Descriptions

Chair Yoga. This gentler form of yoga is practiced sitting on a chair or using a chair for support during standing poses.  Chair yoga helps increase flexibility, lung capacity, circulation and strength, improves balance, and relieves stress.  The class incorporates breathing exercises, stretching, yoga postures, and final relaxation.

Mat / Meditation. An open level vinyasa class followed by a shorter guided meditation. The vinyasa flow aims to prime your body and mind for the meditation practice.  Meditation helps with stress reduction, relaxation, awareness and focus.

Open Level Vinyasa. Expect to move with your breath and focus your attention; this well-balanced flow class will get you out of your head and into your body and your breath. Modifications will be offered for both beginners and advanced practitioners.

Trauma-Informed Yoga. Trauma can leave the body feeling dis-regulated and unsafe. Trauma-informed yoga classes help people explore and identify resources for self-regulation. This class invites you to experience breath and movement in a non-threatening and nurturing environment. Each class combines yoga postures and breathing techniques in a balanced sequence.

Yoga Nidra / Meditation. Some weeks, we will practice Yoga Nidra, and other weeks, we will do guided meditation. Yoga Nidra is the practice and art of Yogic Sleep. Yoda Nidra guides you through a detailed sleep meditation that allows your mind and body to slip into a deep state of relaxation. Meditation helps with stress reduction, relaxation, awareness and focus.
